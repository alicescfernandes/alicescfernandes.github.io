Observador V2

Case Study on how this was built from the ground-up

Why a V2

So, Observador is probably one of the most complex WordPress systems out there. Most of the default WordPress front-end is gone, and was almost fully replace with an over-the-top custom theme.

This makes the development hard, since the website has now more than five years of existence, and the codebase is cluttered with old javascript, old PHP, unfinished features, and old feautures that weren't fully removed.

We also had new projects happening (like radio) and we needed a website that was capable to provide a good experience to our readers, while keeping the same look and feel and the audio playing while navigating.

That being said, we were definitely ready for V2.

The new architecture

Now, since we wanted continous play (of our radio and podcasts) we went for a Single-Page application, which is a type of application that ticked all the boxes of what we needed.

Too sum it up, in our single-page application, the website never never reloads. All the content is downloaded by XHR (AJAX if you fancy jQuery), parsed and replaced by Javascript it a matter of seconds. And when the scripts fails to complete it's mission, you redirected to the page anyway.

We still have back-end running, and we request the pages just like a normal request, but only use a bit of content received

Overall, this proved to be a very technical challenge, as the scripts for each type of content (like articles, pages, automatic grids) still needed to be ran on the respective page, and of course, the events that are attached, but never get removed, so they just keep running.
